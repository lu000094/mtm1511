About Maplebrook Public Library

Serving our community since 1952

Our Story

Maplebrook Public Library has been a cornerstone of our community for over seventy years. What started as a small room with a few hundred books has grown into a modern library serving thousands of residents with books, digital resources, programs, and spaces for everyone.

Today, we continue the tradition of free and open access to information while embracing new technologies and services that meet the changing needs of our community.

Our Mission

To connect our community with the resources, knowledge, and opportunities needed to learn, grow, and thrive. We believe every person deserves access to information, a quiet place to study, and welcoming spaces to gather.

Our Values

Access for All

Free and equitable access to information, resources, and technology for every member of our community.

Lifelong Learning

Supporting education and curiosity at every age, from children's story time to adult learning programs.

Community Connection

Creating welcoming spaces where neighbours can meet, collaborate, and build lasting relationships.

Intellectual Freedom

Protecting the right to read, think, and explore ideas freely without judgment or restriction.

By the Numbers

Our library is a busy place. Here is a snapshot of what we do each year:

  • Over 85,000 items in our collection
  • More than 250,000 visits per year
  • 400+ free programs and events annually
  • 12,000 active library cardholders
  • Free Wi-Fi, 30 public computers, and study rooms available
